Get to Know Your Greens: A Guide to Our Community Plants.

Tomato

Planting Season: Spring to early summer
Growth Time: 60-80 days
Sunlight: Full sun (6-8 hours daily)
Watering: Keep soil consistently moist, water deeply
Tips: Tomatoes thrive with consistent watering and benefit from staking or cages to support heavy fruit.

Watermelon

Planting Season: Late spring to early summer (after the last frost)
Growth Time: 70-90 days
Sunlight: Full sun (8+ hours)
Watering: Water deeply once a week, more often in hot or dry conditions; avoid watering leaves to prevent disease
Tips: Use mulch to keep soil warm and retain moisture.

Kale

Planting Season: Spring or fall
Growth Time: 50-75 days
Sunlight: Partial to full sun
Watering: Moderate, keep soil moist but avoid overwatering
Tips: Harvest leaves from the bottom up; it’s very cold-hardy and can survive frost, making it ideal for multiple seasons.

Carrot

Planting Season: Early spring or late summer
Growth Time: 70-80 days
Sunlight: Full sun (6+ hours)
Watering: Keep soil evenly moist, especially during germination
Tips: Prefers loose, well-drained soil; harvest when roots reach about 1/2 to 1 inch in diameter.
